Overview

Peacocks, scientifically known as Pavo cristatus, are large, colorful birds belonging to the pheasant family, Phasianidae. Native to the Indian subcontinent, peacocks are renowned for their vibrant plumage and elaborate courtship displays. Here are some key aspects of peacocks:

Physical Characteristics

    The males are especially known for their striking appearance. They possess a long, iridescent tail, or "train," which is not actually part of the tail but elongated upper tail coverts.
